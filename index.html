Splash Mural

Paint Together, Create Forever

About

Splash Mural is an innovative collaborative painting experience where multiple players use their mobile devices as virtual paintbrushes to create stunning artwork on a shared canvas. Connect your phone, choose your brush color, and watch as your collective creativity comes to life on the big screen! Whether you're painting abstract masterpieces or working together on coordinated designs, Splash Mural transforms any gathering into an interactive art session.

How to Play

Connecting Your Device

Scan the QR code with your mobile device to join the painting session. Pick your brush color from the palette. Once a maximum of 4 players are connected, the host can begin the painting session!

Brush Controls

Swing your phone in the direction you want to paint, and the brush will follow your movement. Extra strong movements will generate special effects and sounds to enhance the experience.

Creating Together

Work together to create beautiful compositions! Coordinate with other players to paint synchronized patterns, fill in sections, or add details to each other's work. After the painting session is over, the painting (together with the other creations from the session) will be displayed in a gallery on a secondary screen.

Technology

GPU Particle System

To achieve high-performance paint simulation with multiple players painting simultaneously, we developed a GPU-based particle system. By offloading particle calculations to the graphics card, we can render thousands of paint particles in real-time without compromising frame rate, creating smooth and responsive visual feedback that makes the painting experience feel natural and fluid.

Motion-Based Interaction

The mobile controller uses the phone's built-in accelerometer and gyroscope sensors to detect movement and rotation. This intuitive interaction model transforms your smartphone into a natural painting tool—simply swing your device as if you're holding a physical paintbrush. The sensor data is processed in real-time to translate physical gestures into brush strokes, with velocity and direction mapped directly to the visual output.

Development Journey

Challenges & Solutions

One of our biggest technical hurdles was achieving low-latency communication between mobile devices and the main display. Traditional web clients require trusted SSL certificates for WebRTC connections, which created significant delays and complexity in a local network environment.

Our Solution: We developed a creative "gray cloud" architecture that routes traffic through our personal domain. This approach satisfied the browser's certificate requirements while maintaining high-speed local WiFi connections. The result? Near-instantaneous brush response that makes the painting feel direct and responsive, despite the technical constraints.

Coordinating multiple players painting simultaneously while maintaining smooth performance also required careful optimization of our network protocol and rendering pipeline. We implemented efficient state synchronization and predictive interpolation to ensure all players see consistent results without overwhelming the network or GPU.

Inspiration & Related Work

Our project draws inspiration from several successful collaborative gaming experiences. Games like It Takes Two feature memorable collaborative painting sections that demonstrate the joy of creative cooperation. Similarly, Dreams by Media Molecule showcases the potential of motion controllers for artistic expression, while party games like Jackbox demonstrate how smartphones can serve as versatile game controllers.

We also looked to digital art installations and interactive museum exhibits that use motion tracking and collaborative creation to engage audiences. These influences helped shape our vision of making digital painting accessible, social, and fun.

Lessons Learned

Creative Problem-Solving Over Perfect Solutions: Sometimes the "hacky" solution (like our gray cloud approach) is the right one when it unblocks development and meets user needs.

Performance Budgets Are Critical: Early decisions to use GPU particles paid dividends throughout development. Architectural choices made for scalability early on prevented costly rewrites later.

User Testing Reveals Unexpected Behaviors: We discovered that players naturally created their own collaborative painting games and patterns—behavior we didn't anticipate but ultimately embraced in our design.

Motion Controls Require Careful Tuning: The sensitivity and responsiveness of accelerometer-based controls needed extensive iteration. What feels natural to one person might feel too sensitive or sluggish to another.

Local Multiplayer Has Unique Magic: Despite the rise of online gaming, creating something together in the same physical space generates a special energy and social connection that's worth preserving and designing for.
